# Cover letter — Computers & Chemical Engineering

Dear Editor,

Please consider the enclosed manuscript, "A physics-informed, drift-adaptive
soft-sensor framework with calibrated uncertainty under delayed labels: from benchmark
validation to a negative control and production serving," for publication in
*Computers & Chemical Engineering* as a full-length article.

The paper integrates five mechanisms that production soft sensors need but the
literature treats separately — leakage-free model selection under regime shift, drift
correction at the documented label delay, adaptive conformal calibration whose
bookkeeping remains correct when laboratory results arrive late, data-efficient model migration
across operating regimes, and a real-time online implementation — under a single
validation discipline. Three properties distinguish the contribution. First, the
evaluation includes a deliberately hostile negative control (SECOM, no physics
anchors), which isolates *where the performance comes from*: the point sensor fails
(R² = −1.84) while conformal validity holds (coverage 0.910–0.915 at 37% narrower
intervals than a static baseline). Second, every quantitative claim regenerates from
single commands against provenance-stamped evidence artifacts in the public
repository, and the full pipeline reproduced exactly across two machines. Third, the
limits of the methods are stated as results, not hedges — including an analytical and
empirical demonstration that a published migration method degenerates to from-scratch
regression for linear source models.

The manuscript is approximately 7,000 words with seven figures and four tables. It is
the author's original work, is not under consideration elsewhere, and the sole author
meets all CRediT criteria. Suggested reviewer expertise: industrial soft sensing,
conformal prediction for time series, process model migration.

Thank you for your consideration.
