# Cover letter — Journal of Process Control

Dear Editor,

Please consider the enclosed manuscript, "When does a calibrated soft sensor keep its promise? A
negative-control study of validity without accuracy under drift and delayed labels," for
publication in the Journal of Process Control as a full-length article.

I will state its history plainly. The work was first submitted to Computers & Chemical Engineering
(CACE-D-26-00944) and declined at editorial screening on perceived scope and novelty. That decision
was useful: it forced the contribution to be stated as one falsifiable claim rather than an
integration of parts. The manuscript is now built on that claim, and the Journal of Process
Control, whose remit is the methods that keep industrial loops observable and controllable, is its
natural home.

The claim is that validity and accuracy are separable promises a soft sensor makes, and that
conformal validity is the model-agnostic one. A deliberately hostile negative control carries the
evidence. On SECOM, a dataset with no physical anchors, the point estimate fails outright
(R2 = -1.84) while conformal validity survives intact (coverage 0.910 to 0.915, intervals 37%
narrower than a static baseline). The dissociation is the result: physics buys accuracy, calibration
alone buys valid coverage, and the two do not stand or fall together. The same discipline holds
under regime shift, at the documented label delay, and in a real-time implementation whose adaptive
conformal bookkeeping stays correct when laboratory results arrive late.

Every quantitative claim regenerates from single commands against provenance-stamped artifacts in
the public repository, and the pipeline reproduced exactly across two machines. The limits of the
methods are reported as results, including an analytical and empirical demonstration that a
published migration method degenerates to from-scratch regression for linear source models. The
manuscript is the author's original work, is not under consideration elsewhere, and the sole author
meets all authorship criteria. Suggested reviewer expertise: industrial soft sensing, conformal
prediction for time series, and process model migration under regime shift.

Thank you for your consideration.
