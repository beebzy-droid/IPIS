# Cover letter — Computers & Chemical Engineering

Dear Editor,

Please consider the enclosed manuscript, "A composed coverage certificate for closed-loop
process operation: certified joint product-quality and equipment-survival guarantees under
feedback," for publication in Computers & Chemical Engineering as a full-length article.

I will state its history plainly. The manuscript was first submitted to Industrial &
Engineering Chemistry Research (ie-2026-03342s), where the editor judged it clearly presented
and technically sound but outside the interest of that journal's broad general-chemistry
readership, and offered a transfer to ACS Omega. I declined that transfer. The contribution
is a process-systems-engineering result, and Computers & Chemical Engineering is its natural
home: it sits squarely within your scope of mathematical programming and optimization, process
dynamics and control, abnormal-events management and process safety, and plant operations.

The contribution is a guarantee, not a prediction. Soft sensing, prognostics, and real-time
optimization each now ship distribution-free coverage guarantees, but safe closed-loop
operation depends on a joint event none of them addresses alone: the product must stay in
specification while the equipment survives to its next maintenance window. The paper derives a
per-cycle composed coverage certificate for that joint event (Theorem 1), resolves the
long-standing objection that feedback voids conformal validity by showing the selection penalty
is exactly zero under causal timing, and collapses the certificate into a single convex budget
that a modifier-adaptation optimizer enforces directly (Corollary 1), so the flagship guarantee
and the operating floor become the same constraint. A negative control carries the evidence: on
a calibrated debutanizer twin running the real soft-sensor, prognostic, and optimizer code, the
certified floor of 0.75 is met at joint safety coverage 0.988 when the budget is enforced and
collapses to 0.000 when the single causal ingredient is removed, the contrast carried entirely
by reflux-pump life.

The work is the integration keystone of a five-paper program. Its closest companion, the
conformal real-time-optimization back-offs it builds the health-constrained optimizer on, is
under review at this journal (CACE-D-26-01040); the calibrated soft-sensor study is under review
at the Journal of Process Control (JPROCONT-D-26-00618) and the prognostics paper at Reliability
Engineering & System Safety. Every number and figure regenerates from a
single script against the public repository. The manuscript is original, is not under
consideration elsewhere, and the sole author meets all authorship criteria. Suggested reviewer
expertise: conformal prediction under distribution shift, real-time optimization and modifier
adaptation, and prognostics and health management for process equipment.

Thank you for your consideration.
