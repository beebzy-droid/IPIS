# Cover letter — Industrial & Engineering Chemistry Research

Dear Editor,

Please consider the enclosed manuscript, "A composed coverage certificate for
closed-loop process operation: unifying conformal soft sensing, calibrated prognostics,
and health-constrained real-time optimization," for publication in *Industrial &
Engineering Chemistry Research* as a full-length article.

Soft sensors, prognostic models, and real-time optimization (RTO) each now ship
distribution-free coverage guarantees, but safe closed-loop operation depends on a joint
event that none of them addresses alone: the product must stay in specification while the
equipment survives to its next maintenance window. This manuscript derives that joint
guarantee. Three properties distinguish the contribution. First, the certificate is
derived rather than asserted: a per-cycle composed coverage bound whose terms map
one-to-one onto the three modules (Theorem 1), with the long-standing objection that
feedback voids conformal validity resolved by a causal-timing argument that makes the
conformal-selection penalty exactly zero whenever the decision is formed before the
measurement it is conditioned against. Second, the certificate is enforceable as a single
convex constraint, a similitude-departure budget that a modifier-adaptation RTO imposes
directly (Corollary 1), so the flagship guarantee and the operating floor become the same
constraint; on a calibrated debutanizer twin running the real soft-sensor, prognostic, and
RTO implementations, the certified floor of 0.75 is met empirically (joint safety coverage
0.988) when the budget is enforced and collapses to 0.000 when it is not, the entire
contrast carried by reflux-pump life. Third, every number and figure in the paper
regenerates from a single script against provenance-stamped code in the public repository.

The manuscript is approximately 6,000 words with three figures and three tables. It is the
author's original work, is not under consideration elsewhere, and the sole author meets all
CRediT criteria. Suggested reviewer expertise: conformal prediction under distribution
shift, real-time optimization and modifier adaptation, and prognostics and health
management for process equipment.

Thank you for your consideration.
