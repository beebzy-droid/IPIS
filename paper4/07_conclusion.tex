\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

Soft sensing, prognostics, and real-time optimization each ship a calibrated
uncertainty guarantee, but a closed-loop operator needs a guarantee on the joint
system-safety event --- in-specification product \emph{and} surviving equipment ---
and that joint guarantee does not follow from the three taken separately. This paper
supplied it. The per-cycle composed coverage certificate of Theorem~\ref{thm:composed}
states the joint guarantee explicitly, with terms that map one-to-one onto the three
modules, and a causal-timing argument shows that feedback does not void it: the
conformal-selection penalty is exactly zero whenever the decision is formed before the
measurement it is conditioned against. Corollary~\ref{cor:floor} converts the
certificate into a single convex $\psi$-budget that a modifier-adaptation optimizer
enforces directly, so that the flagship guarantee and the operating floor are one and
the same constraint --- the optimizer is forbidden from optimizing so aggressively that
it invalidates its own sensor and life guarantees.

On a calibrated debutanizer twin running the real soft-sensor, prognostic, and
optimization implementations, the certified floor $1-(\alpha_1+\alpha_2)-\varepsilon =
0.75$ was honoured empirically (joint safety coverage $0.988$) under the budget and
collapsed (to $0.000$) without it, the entire contrast carried by reflux-pump life. None
of the three guarantees implies system safety on its own; the composition is the
certificate.

Three directions follow. The per-cycle certificate should be lifted to a horizon
guarantee under adaptive conformal inference and realized on a dynamic high-fidelity
simulator and on plant data, moving from the quasi-static demonstration here to a
physically deployed loop. The Bonferroni composition should be tightened with a
dependence-aware model of the joint soft-sensor/prognostic failure, recovering the
margin that positive failure correlation leaves on the table. And the framework should
be carried from a single column to a plant, with the coverage budget allocated across
units --- the step that turns a certificate for one column into a certificate for an
integrated process.
