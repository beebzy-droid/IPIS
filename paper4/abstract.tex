A self-optimizing plant is safe only when a joint event holds: the product stays in
specification while the equipment survives to its next maintenance window. We show this joint
guarantee can be certified in closed loop, that feedback does not void it, and that one convex
constraint enforces it. Soft sensors, prognostics, and real-time
optimization (RTO) each now ship distribution-free coverage guarantees, yet the joint event that
determines safety follows from none of them alone, and feedback is widely believed to break the
exchangeability conformal validity rests on. We derive a per-cycle composed coverage certificate
for the joint event, its terms mapping onto the three modules plus a selection penalty. A
causal-timing argument shows the selection penalty is exactly zero whenever the decision is formed
before the measurement it is conditioned against, dissolving the feedback objection. A corollary
collapses the certificate into one convex similitude-departure budget a modifier-adaptation RTO
enforces directly, making the flagship guarantee and the operating floor the same constraint. A
negative control isolates the mechanism: on a calibrated debutanizer twin running the real
soft-sensor, prognostic, and RTO code, the certified floor of 0.75 is met at joint safety coverage
0.988 (95\% interval [0.976, 0.994]) when the budget is enforced, and collapses to 0.000 when the
budget, the single causal ingredient, is removed. Reflux-pump life carries the contrast:
remaining-life coverage 1.000 against 0.013. None of the three guarantees implies system safety
alone; the composition, and the budget that enforces it, is the certificate.
