Soft sensors, prognostic models, and real-time optimization (RTO) each increasingly
ship distribution-free coverage guarantees, yet safe closed-loop operation depends on
the joint event that the product is in specification \emph{and} the equipment survives
to its next maintenance window --- a guarantee that does not follow from the three
taken in isolation, and that feedback is widely believed to void by breaking
exchangeability. We derive a per-cycle \emph{composed} coverage certificate for this
joint safety event whose terms map one-to-one onto the three modules. A causal-timing
argument shows the conformal-selection penalty is exactly zero whenever the decision is
formed before the measurement it is conditioned against, dissolving the feedback
objection. A corollary collapses the certificate into a single convex
similitude-departure budget that a modifier-adaptation RTO enforces directly: the
optimizer may not optimize so aggressively that it invalidates its own sensor and life
guarantees, and the flagship guarantee and the operating floor become one constraint.
On a calibrated debutanizer digital twin running the real soft-sensor, prognostic, and
RTO implementations, the certified floor $1-(\alpha_1+\alpha_2)-\varepsilon = 0.75$ is
honoured empirically (joint safety coverage $0.988$, $95\%$ interval $[0.976, 0.994]$)
when the budget is enforced and collapses to $0.000$ when it is not, the contrast
carried entirely by reflux-pump life (remaining-life coverage $1.000$ versus $0.013$).
None of the three guarantees implies system safety on its own; the composition is the
certificate.
