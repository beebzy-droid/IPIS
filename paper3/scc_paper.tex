\title{Similarity-Calibrated Conformal Prediction: a physics-derived, a-priori coverage
certificate for prognostics under operating-regime transfer}
\author{Bien Busico}
\address{Chemical Engineer, Quezon City, Philippines}

\begin{abstract}
Distribution-free uncertainty quantification by conformal prediction guarantees coverage
only under exchangeability, which fails when a prognostic model calibrated on one operating
regime is deployed on another: the remaining-useful-life (RUL) interval silently
miscovers. Existing remedies fall into two disjoint camps---dimensionless transfer learning,
which improves point accuracy but offers no coverage guarantee, and weighted (covariate-shift)
conformal, which restores coverage only by estimating density ratios from target data. We
introduce \emph{Similarity-Calibrated Conformal} (SCC): computing the nonconformity score in
a dimensionless space obtained from the governing physics (the Buckingham $\Pi$ theorem)
restores approximate score-exchangeability across regimes, yielding a coverage certificate
that is computable \emph{a priori from physical parameters alone}, with no target failure data
and no density-ratio estimation. We prove the certificate by composing the dimensionless
reduction with the non-exchangeable conformal bound of Barber et al. (2023): the cross-regime
coverage gap is at most $2L\lVert\Delta\boldsymbol\psi\rVert$ in a similitude-departure
parameter $\boldsymbol\psi$. On a controlled catalyst-deactivation testbed---where dynamic
similitude is genuine but is not assumed by the estimator---naive conformal miscovers (coverage
collapses to 0.58, gap up to 0.34) while SCC recovers coverage to within 0.006 of the target;
under a controlled departure from similitude the gap and the interval back-off degrade
gracefully and monotonically, the a-priori bound upper-bounds the measured gap on 100\% of
held-out configurations, and the departure predicts the score-distribution mismatch with
held-out $R^2=0.79$. A bootstrap similitude diagnostic returns a three-way verdict
(holds/violated/indeterminate) that lets SCC decline to certify outside its operating envelope;
on the FEMTO bearing benchmark it correctly returns \emph{indeterminate}, exposing the method's
applicability limit. The contribution is coverage theory rather than point accuracy;
real-process validation on adequately powered field data is the principal item of future work.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}
Reliable prognostics requires not just a remaining-useful-life (RUL) point estimate but a
calibrated statement of its uncertainty: a maintenance decision rests on a \emph{lower bound}
the true life is unlikely to fall below. Conformal prediction supplies such bounds with a
finite-sample, distribution-free coverage guarantee~\cite{vovk2005algorithmic,papadopoulos2002inductive,lei2018distribution,angelopoulos2023gentle},
which has made it attractive for data-driven RUL estimation~\cite{javanmardi2023conformal}.
That guarantee, however, rests on \emph{exchangeability} of calibration and test data---an
assumption that breaks precisely in the situation reliability engineering cares about most:
a model calibrated on assets run under one operating regime (load, speed, temperature) is
deployed on assets run under another. The degradation distribution shifts, exchangeability
fails, and the conformal interval miscovers without warning.

Two bodies of work bear on this transfer problem, and they do not meet. The first uses
\emph{dimensional analysis}: expressing a model in dimensionless variables (via the Buckingham
$\Pi$ theorem) enforces dynamic similitude between systems and improves out-of-regime
generalization of \emph{point} predictions~\cite{buckinghampi2022ml}. None of this literature
carries a coverage guarantee. The second is \emph{weighted}, or covariate-shift, conformal
prediction~\cite{tibshirani2019conformal} and its non-exchangeable
generalization~\cite{barber2023conformal}, which restore coverage under distribution shift---but
do so by estimating likelihood ratios of the target to the source distribution, which requires
representative target data. For a newly commissioned asset, or a regime with no run-to-failure
history, that data does not exist.

The union of these two ideas is empty, and that gap is the opening. We ask: \emph{can physical
similarity itself deliver a distribution-free coverage guarantee under transfer, without
estimating density ratios?} We answer yes, and call the method Similarity-Calibrated Conformal
(SCC). The idea is to compute the conformal nonconformity score in a dimensionless space chosen
so that, under dynamic similitude, the score distribution is invariant across operating
regimes. Standard conformal needs only the \emph{scores} to be exchangeable; a physics-derived
dimensionless transformation makes them approximately so, and the residual coverage gap is
controlled by the mismatch in a physical, a-priori-computable quantity.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
\textbf{(C1)} Dimensionless ($\Pi$-space) conformal calibration that restores approximate
cross-regime score-exchangeability (\S\ref{sec:method}).
\textbf{(C2)} An \emph{a-priori coverage certificate} (Theorem~\ref{thm:scc},
Corollary~\ref{cor:apriori}): the cross-regime coverage gap is at most
$2L\lVert\Delta\boldsymbol\psi\rVert$ in a similitude-departure parameter computable from
physical parameters, with no target failure data---obtained by composing the dimensionless
reduction with the non-exchangeable bound of~\cite{barber2023conformal}.
\textbf{(C3)} A bootstrap \emph{similitude diagnostic} giving SCC a self-aware operating
envelope (holds / violated / indeterminate), so it declines to certify when its premise is
unsupported (\S\ref{sec:method}).
\textbf{(C4)} A controlled-physics validation protocol that tests the certificate
\emph{non-circularly}, via a graded departure from similitude (\S\ref{sec:design}).

\paragraph{Scope.} We are explicit about what is and is not demonstrated. The guarantee is
conditional on physical hypotheses (H1--H2) that are falsifiable, not assumed true; validation
is on a controlled physical simulation in which similitude is genuine but is not assumed by the
estimator; and the one real-data benchmark (FEMTO) returns \emph{indeterminate}, an honest
applicability limit rather than a positive result. Real-process validation on adequately
powered field data is future work.

\section{Background and related work}\label{sec:background}
\paragraph{Split conformal and its guarantee.} Given a nonconformity score $V=s(X,Y)$ and a
calibration set exchangeable with the test point, the split-conformal prediction set obtained
from the empirical $(1-\alpha)$ score quantile covers the truth with probability at least
$1-\alpha$~\cite{papadopoulos2002inductive,lei2018distribution}. The guarantee is marginal and
finite-sample, and requires no model assumptions---its sole structural requirement is
exchangeability.

\paragraph{Beyond exchangeability.} Barber et al.~\cite{barber2023conformal} bound the coverage
loss when exchangeability fails: the gap is controlled by the total-variation distances between
the score vector and its versions with the test point swapped against each calibration point.
For independent data this reduces to a sum of pairwise total-variation distances between source
and target score laws. We use this bound as the analytical backbone of the SCC certificate.

\paragraph{Covariate-shift conformal.} Weighted conformal~\cite{tibshirani2019conformal} restores
exact coverage under covariate shift using importance weights $w(x)=\mathrm{d}P_T/\mathrm{d}P_S$.
This is powerful but requires estimating the density ratio, hence representative target
covariates. SCC differs in kind: it removes the shift physically (by a dimensionless
transformation) rather than reweighting statistically, and its certificate needs no target data.

\paragraph{Dimensional similitude and transfer.} The Buckingham $\Pi$ theorem reduces a physical
relationship to dimensionless groups; matching groups enforces dynamic similitude. Recent work
exploits this for transfer learning, training networks in dimensionless variables to generalize
across scales and systems~\cite{buckinghampi2022ml}. These methods target point accuracy and
provide no calibrated uncertainty. SCC imports the similitude principle into the conformal
calibration step to obtain a \emph{coverage} guarantee.

\paragraph{Prognostic UQ.} Conformal and related distribution-free methods have been applied to
RUL on run-to-failure benchmarks~\cite{javanmardi2023conformal,lei2018machinery}. Our concern is
orthogonal to point accuracy: the validity of the interval when the operating regime changes
between calibration and deployment.

\section{Method: Similarity-Calibrated Conformal}\label{sec:method}
\paragraph{Setup.} Units are indexed by operating condition $c$ with known physical parameters
$\theta_c$ (load, speed, temperature). A unit yields features $X$ and true RUL $Y$ with joint
law $\mathbb P_c$; a pre-trained predictor $\hat y$ and the one-sided over-prediction score
$V=\hat y(X)-Y$ (a lower RUL bound) have score law $\law c$. Write $\dtv$ for total variation.

\begin{definition}[Dimensionless reduction]\label{def:pi}
The Buckingham $\Pi$ theorem yields dimensionless groups, partitioned into (i) \emph{scale-setting}
groups, used to form a characteristic score scale $\sigma(\theta_c)>0$ such that the
\emph{dimensionless score} $\tilde V=V/\sigma(\theta_c)$ has a condition-invariant law under
exact similitude, and (ii) a \emph{similitude-departure parameter}
$\boldsymbol\psi(\theta_c)\in\mathbb R^m$ collecting residual groups the scale does not absorb
(unmodeled physics). $\boldsymbol\psi$ is constant across conditions when the dimensionless model
is complete. Let $\lawt c$ be the law of $\tilde V$ under $\mathbb P_c$.
\end{definition}

For rolling-contact fatigue (Lundberg--Palmgren / ISO 281~\cite{lundberg1947dynamic,iso281},
rating life $\propto(C/P)^p$, $p=3$) the scale $\sigma=T_L\propto(C/P)^p/n$ \emph{absorbs} the
load--speed group $P/C$. For first-order catalyst deactivation (Arrhenius $k=A e^{-E/RT}$;
\cite{levenspiel1999chemical,perry2008handbook}) the scale $\sigma=1/k_{\mathrm{nom}}$
\emph{contains} the primary rate group $e^{-E/RT}$. In both, large differences in the dominant
operating parameter are absorbed by $\sigma$ and do not by themselves induce score-distribution
mismatch.

\begin{assumption}[Lipschitz similitude departure]\label{ass:lip}
There exists $L<\infty$ with
$\dtv(\lawt c,\lawt{c'})\le L\lVert\boldsymbol\psi(\theta_c)-\boldsymbol\psi(\theta_{c'})\rVert$
for all $c,c'$.
\end{assumption}
The exact-similitude boundary case ($\boldsymbol\psi\equiv\mathrm{const}\Rightarrow\lawt c=\lawt{c'}$,
``H1'') holds \emph{irrespective of how far apart the scale-setting parameters are}; the global
Lipschitz form (``H2'') is the regularity needed away from exact similitude. H1--H2 are physical
hypotheses, not theorems; the diagnostic below tests them.

\paragraph{SCC procedure.} Split conformal in dimensionless coordinates: (1) on calibration
$\{(X_i,Y_i)\}_{i=1}^n$ i.i.d.\ from source $S$, form $\tilde V_i=s(X_i,Y_i)/\sigma(\theta_S)$;
(2) take the conformal quantile $\tilde q=\tilde V_{(\lceil(1-\alpha)(n+1)\rceil)}$; (3) for a
target unit under condition $T$, return $\mathcal C(X_{n+1})=\{y:s(X_{n+1},y)\le\sigma(\theta_T)\tilde q\}$,
re-dimensionalizing the quantile by the \emph{target} scale. Naive conformal is the case
$\sigma\equiv1$ and miscovers whenever $\sigma(\theta_S)\neq\sigma(\theta_T)$.

\begin{lemma}[\cite{barber2023conformal}]\label{lem:barber}
For split conformal with weights $w_i$ and pre-fitted scores, the coverage gap is at most
$(1+\sum_i w_i)^{-1}\sum_i w_i\,\dtv(R(Z),R(Z^i))$, where $R(Z)$ is the score vector and $R(Z^i)$
swaps the test point with point $i$. For independent data,
$\dtv(R(Z),R(Z^i))\le 2\,\dtv(Z_i,Z_{n+1})$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{theorem}[SCC certificate]\label{thm:scc}
Under Assumption~\ref{ass:lip}, with calibration i.i.d.\ from $S$ and target
$(X_{n+1},Y_{n+1})\sim\mathbb P_T$ independent of calibration,
\[
\mathbb P\big(Y_{n+1}\in\mathcal C(X_{n+1})\big)\ge
1-\alpha-\tfrac{2n}{n+1}L\lVert\boldsymbol\psi(\theta_S)-\boldsymbol\psi(\theta_T)\rVert
\ge 1-\alpha-2L\lVert\boldsymbol\psi(\theta_S)-\boldsymbol\psi(\theta_T)\rVert.
\]
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Coverage is $\{\tilde V_{n+1}\le\tilde q\}$ with $\tilde V_{n+1}\sim\lawt T$. Were the
dimensionless scores exchangeable (all $\lawt S$), split conformal gives coverage $\ge1-\alpha$.
Apply Lemma~\ref{lem:barber} with $w_i\equiv1$ (denominator $n+1$). The pre-fitted scores are
independent scalars; swapping calibration point $i$ with the test point exchanges
$\tilde V_i\sim\lawt S$ and $\tilde V_{n+1}\sim\lawt T$ while the other coordinates are unchanged.
The two joint laws are product measures differing in two independent coordinates, so by
subadditivity of TV, $\dtv(R(Z),R(Z^i))\le\dtv(\lawt S,\lawt T)+\dtv(\lawt T,\lawt S)=2\dtv(\lawt S,\lawt T)$.
Summing $n$ terms gives gap $\le\tfrac{2n}{n+1}\dtv(\lawt S,\lawt T)$, and
Assumption~\ref{ass:lip} bounds $\dtv(\lawt S,\lawt T)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[A-priori certificate, two regimes]\label{cor:apriori}
$\lVert\Delta\boldsymbol\psi\rVert$ is a physical quantity, not a function of target failure
data. \emph{(a)} Under complete similitude ($\boldsymbol\psi$ const) the bound is $0$ and
coverage is $\ge1-\alpha$ for arbitrary differences in the scale-setting parameters, requiring
only the target scale $\sigma(\theta_T)$. \emph{(b)} Under incomplete similitude, coverage is
certified up to $2L\lVert\Delta\boldsymbol\psi\rVert$ given a bound on the departure. Neither
regime estimates a density ratio---the property distinguishing SCC from weighted/shift conformal.
\end{corollary}

\paragraph{Estimating $L$.} With $m\ge2$ source conditions, $L$ and a finite-sample intercept
$a$ (the $\dtv$ floor, $a\to0$ as $n^{-1/2}$) are estimated by regressing empirical
$\dtv(\lawt S,\lawt{S'})$ on $\lVert\Delta\boldsymbol\psi\rVert$ over source pairs; the
operational bound $2(a+L\lVert\Delta\boldsymbol\psi\rVert)$ then upper-bounds the target gap. This
uses only source data.

\paragraph{Similitude diagnostic.} Assumption~\ref{ass:lip} bites when $\boldsymbol\psi$ varies
across conditions. Since exact similitude makes the dimensionless life condition-invariant, it is
testable from source data: for each condition pair form $g=\mathrm{median}(D\mid c)/\mathrm{median}(D\mid c')$
where $D$ is the dimensionless life (the dynamic-capacity constant cancels), equivalently the
empirical median-life ratio divided by the L10 prediction. A bootstrap CI on $g$ yields a
three-way verdict: $1$ inside a tight CI $\Rightarrow$ holds; $1$ outside $\Rightarrow$ violated;
CI wider than a preset fold $\Rightarrow$ indeterminate (underpowered). On the latter two SCC
declines the a-priori certificate and defers to data-driven conformal.

\section{Experimental design}\label{sec:design}
\paragraph{Why a controlled testbed.} Demonstrating an a-priori certificate requires data in
which the governing physics actually controls degradation, so that $\boldsymbol\psi$ is
meaningful. We therefore use a stochastic catalyst-deactivation
simulation~\cite{levenspiel1999chemical,perry2008handbook} in which dynamic similitude is
\emph{genuine} but is not assumed by the estimator; the estimator sees only noisy signals and a
nominal rate model. This permits a non-circular test: we impose a graded, physically meaningful
departure from similitude and check that the certificate degrades as predicted.

\paragraph{Simulator.} First-order deactivation $\mathrm{d}a/\mathrm{d}t=-k_{\mathrm{eff}}(T)a$,
failure at $a\le0.3$; primary Arrhenius channel $k_1=A_1 e^{-E_1/RT}$ ($E_1=80$ kJ/mol); an
optional secondary channel $k_2=A_2 e^{-E_2/RT}$ of weight $\eta$ (the controlled departure,
$E_2\neq E_1$). Unit-to-unit variability is a lognormal pre-exponential; the activity signal
carries additive noise. At $\eta=0$ the dimensionless clock $\tau=t\,k_1(T)$ collapses every
temperature onto $a(\tau)=e^{-\tau}$: exact similitude, with the characteristic life
$T_L=1/k_1(T)$ spanning $10.2\times$ across the three temperatures (600/650/700 K).

\paragraph{Predictor, score, metrics.} The predictor extrapolates remaining life from the
observed activity using the nominal rate; the conformal layer covers the residual. We report:
\emph{coverage} (target $1-\alpha=0.90$); \emph{coverage gap} $=\max(0,(1-\alpha)-\text{coverage})$;
\emph{efficiency} as the relative interval back-off $q_T/\overline{\text{RUL}}_T$ (smaller is
more informative; a diverging value signals degenerate intervals); the \emph{operational bound}
$2(a+L\lVert\Delta\boldsymbol\psi\rVert)$; and the \emph{diagnostic} verdict. Numbers are averaged
over five seeds. Three gate criteria were fixed before running: (1) naive miscovers while SCC
recovers at $\eta=0$; (2) the a-priori bound, with $L$ fit on a subset of (pair, $\eta$)
configurations, upper-bounds the gap on \emph{held-out} configurations; (3) the gap degrades
gracefully and monotonically with $\eta$.

\section{Results}\label{sec:results}
\paragraph{Coverage recovery (Fig.~\ref{fig:cov}).} At $\eta=0$, calibrating on one temperature
and deploying on another, naive conformal miscovers badly---coverage collapses to 0.58 when
calibrating on short-lived and deploying on long-lived units (gap up to 0.34), and wastefully
over-covers in the reverse direction. SCC recovers coverage to within 0.006 of the 0.90 target
across all pairs, and Barber's bound $2\dtv(\tilde{\mathcal V})$ contains the SCC gap on every
pair. The dimensionless transform removes the $10.2\times$ scale difference exactly, as
Corollary~\ref{cor:apriori}(a) predicts.

Naive miscovers (or over-covers); SCC tracks the 0.90 target.}\label{fig:cov}

\paragraph{Graceful degradation (Fig.~\ref{fig:dep}).} As the unmodeled secondary channel is
weighted up, the SCC coverage gap rises monotonically (0.006 at $\eta=0$ to 0.103 at $\eta=2$)
and the interval back-off grows from 0.28 to 1.01---coverage is preserved at a quantified
efficiency cost, both degrading predictably. Reporting efficiency alongside coverage rules out
the degenerate regime in which coverage is held by trivially wide intervals.

\paragraph{The a-priori certificate (Fig.~\ref{fig:cert}).} With $a,L$ fit on 60\% of
(pair, $\eta$) configurations, the physical departure $\delta=\eta\lvert h(S)-h(T)\rvert$
predicts the dimensionless score-distribution mismatch on the held-out 40\% with $R^2=0.79$
(correlation 0.95), and the resulting a-priori bound $2(a+L\delta)$ upper-bounds the measured
coverage gap on \emph{100\%} of held-out configurations. The certificate is conservative (it is
an upper bound) and its finite-sample intercept shrinks as $n^{-1/2}$ (from 0.12 at $n=200$ to
0.04 at $n=1600$ per condition).

\paragraph{Robustness (Table~\ref{tab:robust}).} The gate criteria survive across the departure's
temperature-dependence ($E_2=95$--150 kJ/mol at matched magnitude), its magnitude (15--60\%
perturbation), and the coverage target ($\alpha=0.05$/0.10/0.20). At every setting naive
miscovers ($\approx0.13$), SCC recovers ($\approx0.009$), the a-priori bound holds 100\%, the gap
is monotone, and its magnitude orders with the departure's steepness and size---the behavior is a
property of the method, not of one tuning.

\paragraph{Diagnostic and a real-data limit (Fig.~\ref{fig:diag}).} On synthetic data the
diagnostic discriminates correctly: it returns \emph{holds} when lives obey L10, \emph{violated}
when they do not (with adequate units), and \emph{indeterminate} when the within-condition spread
swamps the structural signal. The FEMTO bearing benchmark~\cite{nectoux2012pronostia} falls in
the last category: with $\approx6$ bearings per condition and a $7\times$ within-condition life
spread, the bootstrap CI on $g$ spans a factor of 2.6--4.4, far too wide to resolve the
$1.63\times$ life ratio that L10 predicts between its load/speed conditions. The diagnostic
therefore returns \emph{indeterminate}, and SCC correctly declines its a-priori certificate---an
honest applicability limit, consistent with the dataset authors' report that L10 does not govern
FEMTO lives.

\section{Discussion}\label{sec:discussion}
\paragraph{What the certificate buys.} SCC certifies coverage from physical parameters before any
target failure data exists. Under verified similitude (the diagnostic's \emph{holds}), arbitrarily
large differences in the dominant operating parameters cost nothing, because the dimensionless
scale absorbs them; this is the qualitative content of the $10.2\times$-scale recovery. Under
incomplete similitude the certificate degrades by the departure $\lVert\Delta\boldsymbol\psi\rVert$,
and the diagnostic flags the regime. This is a different guarantee from weighted
conformal~\cite{tibshirani2019conformal}: it trades the need for target data for a falsifiable
physical assumption, which the diagnostic makes checkable.

\paragraph{Limitations.} (i) The guarantee is conditional on H1--H2, physical hypotheses rather
than theorems. (ii) The certificate bounds the gap for the modeled groups; unmodeled physics
contributes a residual that the departure study quantifies and the diagnostic detects, but that
must be bounded a priori to use Corollary~\ref{cor:apriori}(b). (iii) Validation is on controlled
simulation; the single real benchmark returns indeterminate by design rather than a positive
result. (iv) The bound is conservative. The decisive next step is validation on adequately
powered run-to-failure field data, where the diagnostic returns \emph{holds} and the certificate
can be checked against measured cross-regime coverage---data the authors expect to obtain from an
industrial process.

\section{Conclusions}\label{sec:conclusions}
We introduced Similarity-Calibrated Conformal prediction, which calibrates conformal RUL bounds in
a physics-derived dimensionless space to obtain an a-priori, data-free coverage certificate for
transfer across operating regimes---filling the gap between dimensionless transfer learning (no
coverage guarantee) and weighted conformal (needs target data). The certificate follows by
composing the dimensionless reduction with the non-exchangeable conformal bound. On a controlled
catalyst-deactivation testbed the method recovers coverage where naive conformal fails, degrades
gracefully under a controlled departure from similitude with the a-priori bound holding on all
held-out configurations, and is robust across departure structure, magnitude, and coverage level.
An accompanying similitude diagnostic gives the method a self-aware operating envelope and, on the
FEMTO benchmark, correctly identifies that benchmark as outside it. The contribution is coverage
theory; its translation to certified field deployment awaits adequately powered real-process data.
