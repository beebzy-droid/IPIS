\title{Similarity-Calibrated Conformal Prediction: a physics-derived, a-priori coverage
certificate for prognostics under operating-regime transfer}
\author{Bien Busico}
\address{Chemical Engineer, Quezon City, Philippines}

\begin{abstract}
Conformal prediction guarantees coverage only under exchangeability, which fails when a
prognostic model calibrated on one operating regime is deployed on another: the
remaining-useful-life (RUL) interval silently miscovers. Existing remedies are disjoint:
dimensionless transfer learning improves point accuracy without a coverage guarantee, while
weighted conformal restores coverage only by estimating density ratios from target data. We
introduce \emph{Similarity-Calibrated Conformal} (SCC): computing the nonconformity score in a
physics-derived dimensionless space (the Buckingham $\Pi$ theorem) restores approximate
score-exchangeability across operating regimes, yielding a coverage certificate computable
\emph{a priori from physical parameters alone}, with no target failure data. The certificate
composes the dimensionless reduction with the non-exchangeable bound of Barber et al. (2023): the
cross-regime coverage gap is at most $2L\lVert\Delta\boldsymbol\psi\rVert$ in a
similitude-departure parameter. On a controlled catalyst-deactivation testbed in which similitude
is genuine but is not assumed by the estimator, naive conformal miscovers (coverage falls to 0.58)
while SCC recovers it to within 0.006 of target; under a controlled departure the gap and interval
back-off degrade gracefully and monotonically, the a-priori bound holds on 100\% of held-out
configurations, and physical departure predicts the score-distribution mismatch ($R^2=0.79$). A
bootstrap similitude diagnostic returns a three-way verdict (holds/violated/indeterminate) that
lets SCC decline to certify outside its operating envelope; on the FEMTO bearing benchmark it
correctly returns \emph{indeterminate}, exposing the method's applicability limit. The
contribution is coverage theory; real-process validation on adequately powered field data is
future work.
\end{abstract}
