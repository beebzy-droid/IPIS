\section{Conclusions}\label{sec:conclusions}
We presented Similarity-Calibrated Conformal prediction, a deployable method that delivers reliable
remaining-useful-life intervals when an asset is operated in a regime different from the one its model
was calibrated on. By calibrating the conformal quantile in a physics-derived dimensionless
coordinate, SCC restores coverage under operating-regime transfer with no target failure data, fills
the gap between dimensionless transfer learning (which lacks a coverage guarantee) and weighted
conformal (which needs target data), and comes with two features a practitioner can act on: an
a-priori coverage bound computable from physical parameters, and a runtime diagnostic that gives the
method a self-aware operating envelope. On a controlled catalyst-deactivation reliability testbed the
method recovers coverage where regime-blind conformal fails, degrades gracefully under a controlled
departure from similitude with the a-priori bound holding on all held-out configurations, and is
robust across the structure and magnitude of the departure and the coverage level. On the FEMTO
benchmark the diagnostic correctly places that dataset outside the method's envelope, demonstrating
the intended fault-tolerant behaviour. The method is ready for the decisive test, validation on
adequately powered run-to-failure field data from an operating process, which is the focus of
continuing work.
